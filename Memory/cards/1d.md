# Ciliates are a model for comparing transcriptionall active/inactive chromatin

Due to their fairly unique reproductive cycle comprised of a transcriptionally active
macronucleus that handles the phenotype and expresses genes and a
transcriptionally inactive "germline" micronucleus which passes on the genetic
material to the progeny, ciliates are a fantastic model to compare
transcriptionally active and inactive chromatin. One example is the genus
Tetrahymena, which was used to discover telomeres, telomerase and H3K4me
epigenetic marks.

Q: Why are ciliates a good model for studying transcriptionally active chromatin?
A: They have two nuclei, the micronucleus and the macronucleus - the
micronucleus is transcriptionally inactive whereas the macronucleus is
transcriptionally active
